Research Question & Context

Which ground-mounted PV orientation maximises 20-year revenue potential under the German EEG Direktvermarktung framework? This study analyses six bifacial configurations — vertical 90° east/west-facing and conventional 30° south-facing — at two capacity thresholds in Bavaria over a 2026–2045 horizon, quantifying how orientation, EEG §35a capacity thresholds, and §51 curtailment interact to determine long-run revenue potential for agri-PV and ground-mounted installations.

Methodology
  • PV Simulation: PV*SOL Premium with DWD Nürnberg Station hourly irradiance data (8,784 hours, 2024); all systems bifacial, ground-mounted, Bavaria
  • Market Data: SMARD Day-Ahead prices + netztransparenz.de Monatsmarktwert 2024; prices projected across full 2026–2045 horizon
  • Revenue Metric: SUM PV — discounted cumulative 20-year revenue; CAPEX excluded for orientation-only comparison
  • Financial Parameters: 4% discount rate  ·  LUXOR Solar datasheet degradation: 1.0% Year 1, then 0.345%/year
  • Scenarios: With curtailment (§51 EEG compliance — zero revenue at negative Day-Ahead prices) and without curtailment, both with full degradation

Key Findings
Revenue Advantage Over Baseline
+ € 582,768
W90_999kWp outperforms the S30_1MWp baseline by 54.9% in 20-year discounted revenue — driven by its evening-peak generation profile complementing morning load and the preferential EEG agri-PV tariff.
EEG §35a Capacity Threshold Value
≈ € 267,000
Keeping capacity below 1 MW (999 kWp) unlocks a 95.0 vs. 82.3 €/MWh tariff differential — worth approximately €267,000 in additional revenue for the west configuration over 20 years.
Orientation Value — Policy-Independent
+ 30.6%
Under the "Basic" scenario (all systems at 70.3 €/MWh, EEG tariff advantage neutralised), W90_999kWp still leads by +30.6%, confirming orientation is the primary value driver independent of policy.
§51 EEG Curtailment Effect
Enforcing §51 curtailment (zero revenue at negative Day-Ahead prices) improves revenue across all systems by +1.2% to +2.5% by eliminating negative-price losses — a counterintuitive result with direct implications for grid-coupled agri-PV design and EEG policy planning.
Scenarios Analysed
Primary: §51 curtailment applied · LUXOR degradation (1.0% Yr 1 → 0.345%/yr)
Secondary: No §51 curtailment · same degradation profile
Basic: Uniform AW 70.3 €/MWh · curtailment applied · degradation applied
Conclusion
Vertical west-facing bifacial systems at sub-1 MW scale outperform conventional south-facing installations by 54.9% in 20-year revenue under the German EEG framework. The combination of a 95.0 €/MWh agrivoltaic tariff and a morning-load-complementary generation profile makes this configuration strategically superior for both landowners and grid operators.
