Research Question & Context

Which ground-mounted PV orientation maximises 20-year revenue potential under the German EEG Direktvermarktung framework? This study analyses six bifacial configurations — vertical 90° east/west-facing and conventional 30° south-facing — at two EEG §35a capacity thresholds (999 kWp and 1,001 kWp) in Bavaria, over a full 2026–2045 horizon. By combining PV*SOL energy yield simulations with SMARD market data and netztransparenz.de Monatsmarktwert projections, the study quantifies how orientation, EEG tariff structure, and §51 negative-price curtailment together determine long-run revenue potential for agrivoltaic and commercial ground-mounted installations.

Methodology
  • PV Simulation: PV*SOL Premium with DWD Nürnberg Station hourly irradiance (8,784 hours, 2024); all six systems bifacial ground-mounted in Bavaria; rear-side bifacial gain and soiling losses included
  • Market Data: SMARD Day-Ahead prices + netztransparenz.de Monatsmarktwert 2024; Marktprämie per hour = (AW − MMW) × eligible MWh where AW > MMW; price series projected across full 2026–2045 horizon
  • Revenue Metric: SUM PV — discounted present value of cumulative 20-year Direktvermarktung revenue (spot + Marktprämie); CAPEX excluded to isolate orientation-driven revenue differences
  • Financial Parameters: 4% discount rate · LUXOR Solar datasheet degradation: 1.0% in Year 1, then 0.345%/year linear for remaining 19 years
  • Scenarios: With §51 EEG curtailment (zero revenue at negative Day-Ahead prices) and without curtailment; degradation applied identically in both scenarios across all six configurations

Key Findings
Revenue Advantage Over Baseline
+ € 582,768
W90_999kWp outperforms S30_1MWp by 54.9% over 20 discounted years. West-facing panels peak in the evening, when Day-Ahead prices are structurally higher than midday — amplifying revenue at comparable energy yields. Combined with the 95.0 €/MWh agri-PV EEG tariff, this creates a compounding dual advantage over south-facing alternatives.
EEG §35a Capacity Threshold Value
≈ € 267,000
Keeping installed capacity below 1 MW (999 kWp) unlocks the agrivoltaic EEG tariff of 95.0 vs. 82.3 €/MWh for ≥1 MW systems. This single project-sizing decision — the difference between a 999 kWp and 1,001 kWp installation — is worth approximately €267,000 in additional discounted revenue for the west configuration over 20 years.
Orientation Value — Policy-Independent
+ 30.6%
Under the "Basic" scenario — all six systems at the same 70.3 €/MWh EEG reference tariff, removing the agrivoltaic policy advantage entirely — W90_999kWp still leads by +30.6%. Orientation-driven market timing, not EEG tariff structure alone, is the primary revenue driver.
§51 EEG Curtailment Effect
Applying §51 curtailment — zero revenue at negative Day-Ahead prices — improves revenue by +1.2% to +2.5% across all configurations. Selling at negative prices actively reduces cumulative earnings — curtailment prevents those losses. This counterintuitive result has direct implications for inverter-level dispatch logic in grid-coupled agri-PV projects.

Conclusion
Vertical west-facing bifacial systems at sub-1 MW scale outperform conventional south-facing installations by 54.9% in 20-year discounted revenue under the German EEG framework. The combination of a 95.0 €/MWh agrivoltaic tariff and a morning-load-complementary generation profile makes this configuration strategically superior for both landowners and grid operators — and a compelling case for agri-PV adoption in post-EEG transition planning.
